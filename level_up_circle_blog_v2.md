## What Is a Level-Up Circle?

A Level-Up Circle is a radial diagram where each point represents a goal or focus area in your life. The connections between them show how progress in one area **compounds growth** in another.

It's not a to-do list. It's a **momentum machine**.

The concept draws from three proven strategic frameworks:

- **The Flywheel Effect** (Jim Collins, *Good to Great*) — Success isn't one big push. It's small wins that compound until the whole system spins under its own power.
- **Synergy Mapping** (Martin Eppler, 1998) — Place goals in a radial pattern to reveal where they reinforce or conflict with each other.
- **The Hedgehog Concept** (Jim Collins) — Focus on the intersection of what you're passionate about, what you can be best at, and what drives your economic engine.

Disney used this thinking to build an empire. Amazon used it to build a trillion-dollar flywheel. Now you can use it for your life.

## The Science Behind the Circle

This isn't just a pretty diagram. The Level-Up Circle works because it maps to how **compound systems** actually behave.

### Small Wins Create Big Momentum

Jim Collins studied companies that went from good to great. The common pattern? No single defining moment. Instead, relentless small pushes in the same direction — each one building on the last — until the flywheel gained unstoppable momentum.

Your goals work the same way. When exercise improves your energy, that energy boosts your career performance, which fuels your income, which lets you invest more in health. **Each spoke accelerates the wheel.**

### Visualization Changes Behavior

Research consistently shows that people who **see** their goals in context — not just list them — make better decisions about where to invest their time. A Level-Up Circle makes the invisible connections visible. You can literally see which goal, if improved, would cascade into the most others.

### Conflicts Become Obvious

When your goals are scattered across notebooks, apps, and sticky notes, conflicts hide. Put them in a circle with connections drawn, and the tensions become impossible to ignore. That goal that doesn't connect to anything? It might be draining energy from the system.
