The Thinking Behind the Circle

A modern synthesis of classic strategic frameworks, adapted for anyone who wants their goals to reinforce each other.

1 The Flywheel Effect

Jim Collins — Good to Great

Success doesn't come from a single breakthrough. It comes from cumulative momentum — small wins that compound on each other until the whole system spins under its own power.

Each turn of the flywheel builds upon work done earlier, compounding your investment of effort.

The Level-Up Circle converts your goals into a momentum machine. The output of one area — say, fitness — becomes fuel for another, like professional productivity. Over time, every spoke accelerates the wheel.

2 Synergy Mapping

Martin Eppler — 1998

A flat to-do list treats goals as independent items. A Synergy Map places them in a radial pattern and maps their interdependencies — revealing where goals support or conflict with one another.

The Level-Up Circle borrows this visual logic. Instead of a linear checklist, you see a timing circuit of short-term and long-term objectives interacting in real-time.

3 The Hedgehog Concept

Jim Collins — Good to Great

Focus on the intersection of three things:

What you are deeply passionate about.
What you can be the best in the world at.
What drives your economic engine.

When your circle's focus areas sit at that intersection, the flywheel doesn't just spin — it accelerates.
